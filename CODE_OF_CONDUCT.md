# Code of Conduct

Clavix is a small open-source project. The rules are simple:

- **Be respectful.** Disagreements about code, design, or priorities
  are expected and welcome; personal attacks are not.
- **Assume good faith.** Most misunderstandings come from text being
  terser than intended. Ask for clarification before taking offence.
- **Stay on topic.** Issues and pull requests are about Clavix, not
  about unrelated political debates.
- **Keep it legal and safe.** Do not post credentials, proofs of
  concept of unfixed vulnerabilities, or anything that could harm
  users of Clavix or of upstream projects (Vaultwarden, Bitwarden).
- **Security disclosure:** use private channels, not public issues —
  see [DISCLAIMER.md](DISCLAIMER.md) and
  [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md).

The maintainer reserves the right to remove comments, close issues,
or block accounts that repeatedly break these rules. Appeals can be
sent privately to the maintainer via the same channel used for
security reports.

If you are looking for a longer, more formal text, the
[Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/)
expresses the same spirit in more detail and is considered a
compatible reference.
